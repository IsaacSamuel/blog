Faux Maturity

Symptoms of faux maturity:

  • Avoiding things you’d find fun because they are not what an “adult” does. (E.g. Dyeing your hair purple, playing video games.)
  • Doing things you hate because they ARE what an “adult” does. (Buying a house, tending to a lawn, smoking, drinking coffee or alcohol.)
  • Shaming or thinking less of people for doing harmless but weird or non-age-typical things. (Scoffing at someone for watching cartoons or putting M&Ms on their pizza.)
  • Dismissing idealism out of hand–always taking a cynical tact. (“Why are you theorizing about novel forms of government, Mr. Thomas Jefferson?")
  • Scorning goofiness, curiosity, weirdness, and wonder.
March 5, 2018

Anthropological Anxiety

Isaac’s mandatory mental checklist whilst hanging out with his (majority desi / south asian) friends:

  1. Make an extremely convoluted conclusion about desi culture.
  2. What observations cause you to draw this conclusion?
  3. What assumptions are supporting the way you notice and interpret those actions?
  4. In what scenarios could those assumptions possibly be legitimate or illegitimate, and is that even a clearly defined boundary?
  5. Am I overthinking this?
  6. I’m overthinking this.
  7. This is not how a normal person hangs out with their friends.

(Thankfully a large percentage of them speak Hindi half the time, so I have a lot of time to stare at the wall and think.)
